Senior Research Scientist, Amazon

I am a Senior Research Scientist at Amazon, where I work on decision-making under uncertainty using reinforcement learning. My work is typically focused on optimizing real, large-scale supply chains, but I’m broadly interested in reinforcement learning—both single-agent and multi-agent—for large-scale systems.

Research

My work has primarily focused on inventing and deploying novel algorithms for supply chains. Traditional methods in reinforcement learning methods fail to account important facets of real world systems. My work aims at filling in gaps around safe exploration, decision making with operational constraints, sim-to-real transfer, and integration with traditional operations research based solvers and software stacks to deploy novel solutions to production. Beyond real-world reinforcement learning, I'm also generally interested in simulator construction (specifically differentiability), neural approximators for reinforcement learning, meta-learning, and open-ended learning.
